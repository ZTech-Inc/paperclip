### Technical SEO

**Foundation (Month 1):**
- Site architecture: flat URL structure with clear hierarchy (/services/ai-automation, /blog/category/ai-automation)
- Schema markup: Organization, Service, Article, FAQ, HowTo on relevant pages
- Core Web Vitals: LCP < 2.5s, FID < 100ms, CLS < 0.1
- XML sitemap, robots.txt, canonical tags
- Mobile-first responsive design

**Ongoing:**
- Internal linking strategy: every new blog post links to 2-3 related posts and 1 service page
- Page speed monitoring and optimization (monthly)
- Crawl error monitoring via Google Search Console (weekly)
- Structured data validation (monthly)
- 404 monitoring and redirect management

**Link Building:**
- Guest posting on industry publications (2-4 per month): target AI/tech blogs, startup media, blockchain publications
- Digital PR: pitch data-driven stories to journalists (e.g., "Our analysis of 500 smart contract audits reveals...")
- Resource page link building: get listed on "best AI automation tools" and "blockchain development agencies" roundups
- Partner co-marketing: joint content with technology partners that includes backlinks

| Domain Rating (Ahrefs) | 20 | 35 | 50 |

## 3. Social Proof

### Case Studies

**Structure (for each case study):**
1. Client profile (industry, size, challenge)
2. The problem (specific pain point with quantified impact)
3. ZTech's approach (which services, how Paperclip was used)
4. Results (hard metrics: cost reduction %, time saved, revenue impact)
5. Client quote / testimonial
6. Paperclip dashboard screenshot showing real-time transparency

**Publishing Cadence:**
- 1 case study per month minimum
- Distributed across: website, blog, LinkedIn, email newsletter, sales deck
- Video testimonial version for high-value case studies

**Target by Quarter:**
| Quarter | Case Studies | Industries Covered |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| Q1 | 2-3 | E-commerce, SaaS |
| Q2 | 3-4 | Fintech, Healthcare, Professional Services |
| Q3 | 4-5 | All core verticals represented |
| Q4 | 5+ | Deep-dive series on largest engagements |

**Collection Process:**
- COO sends NPS survey at day 30 and day 90 of every engagement
- Clients scoring 9-10 are asked for a written testimonial and optional video
- Testimonials are published on website, LinkedIn, and used in proposals
- Performance Marketer incorporates top testimonials into ad creative

### Certifications & Trust Signals

**Pursue:**
- SOC 2 Type II compliance (signals data security maturity to enterprise clients)
- Google Partner certification (for digital marketing credibility)
- Blockchain ecosystem certifications (Chainlink Build, Alchemy Partner, etc.)

**Display on Website:**
- Client logos (with permission) in a "Trusted By" section
- Certification badges in footer and service pages
- Aggregate metrics: "X workflows automated," "Y smart contracts audited," "$Z in client revenue generated"

## 5. CMO Paperclip Agent -- Campaign Management & Reporting

### How the CMO Agent Operates

The CMO is a Paperclip agent (`claude_local` adapter) that manages ZTech's entire marketing function through Paperclip's task system. Here is how it orchestrates campaigns:

**Campaign Launch Workflow:**
```
CEO assigns: "Launch Q2 demand gen campaign for AI Automation"
  |
  CMO creates Project: "Q2 AI Automation Demand Gen"
  |
  CMO creates sub-issues:
    -> "Develop campaign messaging and creative brief" -> assigns to Content Strategist
    -> "Set up Google Ads campaigns (3 ad groups, 5 ads each)" -> assigns to Performance Marketer
    -> "Optimize landing pages for target keywords" -> assigns to SEO Specialist
    -> "Build landing page" -> cross-team task to CTO
  |
  Each agent executes via heartbeats:
    Content Strategist writes copy, posts deliverable as comment, marks done
    Performance Marketer sets up campaigns, reports initial metrics
    SEO Specialist audits and optimizes pages
    CTO's engineer builds the landing page
  |
  CMO reviews all deliverables via heartbeat:
    Approves or requests revisions via comments
    Monitors performance metrics
    Adjusts strategy based on data
  |
  CMO reports to CEO:
    Weekly: campaign status + key metrics
    Monthly: full performance report with recommendations
```

**Ongoing Campaign Management:**
- CMO heartbeat fires every 1-2 hours during active campaigns
- Each heartbeat: review performance data, identify issues, delegate adjustments
- Performance Marketer runs daily optimization (bid adjustments, budget pacing)
- SEO Specialist monitors rankings weekly, updates content monthly
- Content Strategist maintains publishing cadence per content calendar

### Reporting to COO / CEO

**Weekly Report (generated by CMO agent):**
- Pipeline: new leads, discovery calls scheduled, proposals sent
- Channel performance: impressions, clicks, conversions by channel
- Content: posts published, traffic, engagement
- Budget: spend vs. plan, CPA by channel
- Action items for next week

**Monthly Report (generated by CMO agent, reviewed by CEO):**
- Full funnel analysis: visitors -> leads -> MQLs -> SQLs -> clients
- Channel ROI: CAC and LTV by acquisition channel
- Content performance: top posts by traffic/conversions, keyword ranking changes
- Competitive landscape: competitor marketing moves observed
- Budget reconciliation: actual vs. planned spend, recommended reallocations
- Strategic recommendations for next month

**Quarterly Business Review (CEO + CMO + CFO):**
- Revenue attributed to marketing
- CAC trend and LTV:CAC ratio
- Brand awareness metrics (search volume for branded terms, social following growth)
- Partnership pipeline and referral program performance
- Annual plan progress and adjustments

| Metric | Definition | Target |

**Retention Metrics:**
| Metric | Definition | Target |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Client retention (month 4+) | % of clients continuing past initial 3-month term | >80% |
| Net Revenue Retention | Revenue from existing clients vs. prior period | >110% |

### Dashboard & Tools

**Analytics Stack:**
- **Website analytics:** GA4 with custom event tracking, conversion goals
- **SEO tracking:** Ahrefs or Semrush for keyword rankings, backlink monitoring, competitor analysis
- **Paid ads:** Google Ads + LinkedIn Campaign Manager native dashboards
- **CRM:** HubSpot or Pipedrive for pipeline tracking, lead scoring, attribution
- **Social media:** Native analytics + Buffer or Hootsuite for scheduling and reporting

All marketing data flows back into the CMO agent's context via Paperclip task comments and external tool integrations, enabling data-driven decision-making on every heartbeat.
